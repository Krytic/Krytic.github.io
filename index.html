Sean Richards

MSc Student · Astrophysicist · Aspiring Science Communicator

MSc Student

As an undergrad, I studied Computer Science & Physics at the University of Auckland. In Computer Science, I studied computability theory, algorithmics, and the fundamentals of how computers work. In Physics, I studied astronomy & astrophysics, with a light amount of photonics and quantum mechanics.

Now, as an MSc Student, I'm studying binary star systems using BPASS, and specifically NS-NS mergers as progenitors for gravitational wave transients.

Astrophysicist

I studied a Postgraduate Diploma in Science at Te Punaha Ātea — Auckland Space Institute, producing the dissertation "University of Auckland Ground Station One". This involved upgrading the University's satellite ground station to serve as a viable drop-in replacement for tracking the CubeSat mission APSS-I.

As a MSc by research student, I am examining binary star populations to discover the origins of gravitational wave transient events. I am looking at what factors of a system influence the event rates of transients, and probing the final moments of systems prior to merger.

Aspiring Science Communicator

I am interested in bringing science — and in particular, astronomy — to the masses. Astronomy is one of the oldest sciences, and humans have harbored a deep fascination with the stars since time immemorial. In the words of Carl Sagan, it has been said that astronomy is a humbling and character-building experience. That is, without a doubt, 100% true.
